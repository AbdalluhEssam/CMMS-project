Hospital Preventive Maintenance ( Ventilator )

# Part/Accessory Procedure Check
1 Breating Circut (including inspiratory filter, Flow sensor, Expiratory valve housing and membrance). Replace with sterilized or new signal-patient use parts, Run the tightness test and Flow sensor test, as requierd
2 Brithing Circuit. Empty any waterfrom hoses or water traps, Inspect parts from damage, Replace as necessary.
3 Air-inlet water trap, Gas-inlet water trap. Empty any water.
4 Air-inlet self-emptying water trap (where fitted). None.
5 Fan filter (rear panel). Check for dust and lint. If needed, clean or replace. (Details in operating manual.)
6 Oxygen cell. Replace if exhausted. If exhausted, one of the following messages appears on screen during normal operation: (1- O2 Cell defective. 2- No O2 Cell in use.)

Hospital Preventive Maintenance (Ventilator)

Check
General maintenance of water traps. 1-Inspect the microfilters on the water traps.
2-Replace the microfilters if dirty or discolored.
3-Clean the filter housing if necessary.
Check
Internal cable and tubing checks. 1-Disconnect all tubing and cables (pneumatic and electric) from the PAPHAEL.
2-Using a 2.5mm hexagonal (Allen) key, Remove the 8 screws that secure, Four screws are at the rear of RAPHEAL, Four ate underneath.
3-Remove the closure.
4-Verify that all cables and tubing in RAPHAEL are in good condition, Connected, and Right place.
5-Make sure tha tubing for the oxygen cell measurment is not squeezed between the tank and the white inspiratory tube.
6-Check all assemblies for damage and incorrect installation.
Check
External checks. 1-Make sure the Expiratory valve ring turns smoothly and that the valve plunger pin is clean and moves up and down easily.
2-Make sure the oxygen cell holder is in place and the O-ring is present.
3-Make sure the air and oxygen inlet connectors are mounted correctly.
4-Go to section 9, Backup Battery and voltage tests.
Check
Backup Battery replacement. Checking the age of the backup battery.
Check
12V Batteries condition test. In this test, you measure battery voltage against time (The absolute battery voltage after a first, 2-minutes period of battery use. This voltage must be greater than, or equal to 23.5V).
The drop in battery voltage after a second, 3-minutes period of battery use. This drop must be less than, or equal to (0.5V).
